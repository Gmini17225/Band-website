Festival Adjudicators

Our adjudicators are experienced conductors and music educators who work with each ensemble to offer supportive, practical feedback.

Throughout the festival they listen to performances, lead workshops, and share ideas to help students and teachers grow.

Scott Leithead

Scott Leithead is a choral director and clinician known for his work with youth choirs across Canada and abroad. He has founded and directed several award-winning vocal ensembles and frequently appears as a guest conductor and workshop leader.

Scott is especially passionate about helping young singers develop confidence, musical expression, and a sense of community through choral music. His rehearsals emphasize healthy vocal technique, listening skills, and expressive performance.

Dr. Wendy Zander

Dr. Wendy Zander is a music educator, conductor, and clinician with extensive experience working with school bands and choirs. She has taught at both the secondary and post-secondary levels and has presented at conferences on music education and conducting.

Her work focuses on building solid fundamentals while encouraging students to take musical risks in a supportive environment. At the festival she offers detailed comments, coaching sessions, and clinics tailored to each ensemble.

Kathie Van Lare

Kathie Van Lare is a veteran band conductor and adjudicator with decades of experience leading concert bands and wind ensembles. She has directed school and community groups and has been invited to festivals and honor band events across the country.

Kathie is known for her clear conducting, musical interpretation, and ability to connect with young players. Her feedback highlights both strengths and next steps, helping ensembles refine tone, balance, and overall ensemble musicianship.

Working with the Adjudicators

Each ensemble receives written and recorded comments in addition to an on-stage or clinic-style workshop. Directors are encouraged to bring questions about literature choices, rehearsal strategies, and long-term goals for their programs.
